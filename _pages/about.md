I am a Postdoctoral Associate in the Department of Physics at Virginia Tech, 
working with Prof. Ed Barnes and Prof. Sophia Economou.

My research lies at the intersection of quantum algorithms, quantum simulation, 
condensed-matter physics, and nonequilibrium quantum dynamics. I am particularly 
interested in developing resource-efficient quantum algorithms for dynamical 
correlators, time-dependent Hamiltonians, and periodically driven quantum systems.

## Research Interests

- Quantum algorithms for dynamical correlation functions
- Early and fully fault-tolerant quantum algorithms
- Quantum simulation of many-body systems
- Floquet and nonequilibrium quantum systems
- Variational quantum algorithms
- Tensor-network methods

## Current Research

I am developing SPICE-Q, a sampling-based quantum algorithm for estimating 
multipoint dynamical correlators using a single ancilla and 
precision-independent coherent circuit depth.

I have also developed Floquet-ADAPT-VQE, a variational quantum algorithm for 
computing quasienergies and preparing Floquet states of periodically driven 
quantum systems.

## Background

I received my Ph.D. in Physics from Indiana University Bloomington, where I 
worked with Prof. Babak Seradjeh on nonequilibrium dynamics, Floquet topology, 
and periodically driven many-body systems.
